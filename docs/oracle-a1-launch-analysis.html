ORACLE-A1 Launch Analysis

This note tests whether an independent arbitrager can profitably correct a materially wrong Zoltar WETH/REP OpenOracle report using currently executable Uniswap V3 liquidity. It is a launch-gate measurement, not a timeless proof.

Decision: fail; deployment blocked. At Ethereum block 25,600,852, a minimum-sized report that is wrong by 5% is not profitable to correct in either direction. The only liquid REP/WETH V3 pool is too shallow. This evidence does not substantiate ORACLE-A1.

Method and exact economics

The test reads pools from the canonical Uniswap V3 factory, rejects zero-liquidity pools, compares the current tick with a 30-minute time-weighted average tick, and calls QuoterV2 for exact-input and exact-output amounts. This follows the official V3 oracle, deployment, and QuoterV2 interface documentation.

Let W and R be the current report's WETH and REP amounts; f and p are reporter and protocol fee rates over precision P = 10,000,000. Integer division floors exactly as the contract does. The OpenOracle parameter table remains the canonical owner of configured fee, multiplier, target-error, and sizing values.

Wrong report OpenOracle action Profit before capital cost
REP is cheap: report offers too much REP per WETH Swap token1; fund W + floor(Wf/P) + floor(Wp/P), receive R, then sell REP V3ExactInput(R REP) - WETH contribution - gas
REP is expensive: report offers too little REP per WETH Swap token2; buy and fund R + floor(Rf/P) + floor(Rp/P), receive W W - V3ExactOutput(REP contribution) - gas

The replacement WETH amount is min(floor(W × multiplier / 100), escalationHalt) before the halt, or W + 1 at the halt. Replacement capital is locked, but is not treated as a purchase expense because it is returned to the final reporter. A production decision must separately price that lock duration and the possibility of another dispute.

Approximate break-even is not reached until the cheap-REP report is nearly 100% wrong (around 4,200 REP) or the expensive-REP report is about 33% wrong (around 1,400 REP), before gas and inventory risk. Therefore the configured 5% target error does not imply a profitable 5% correction in the actual venue.

Launch gate and required remediation

ORACLE-A1 passes only if every row of the gate below passes in both error directions. The protocol risk owner owns these thresholds and must sign any change before the deployment decision is rerun.

Gate input Required value Pass rule
Base fee 0, 1, 5, 10, 20, 50, 100, and 200 gwei Every point passes
Open interest 0 and the maximum open interest permitted by launch policy If no maximum is approved and enforced, fail
Error direction REP 5% cheap and REP 5% expensive Both pass at every gas/OI pair
Profit buffer After OpenOracle fees, executable V3 price impact, and gasCost = 600,000 × (2 × baseFee + 2 gwei), multiplied in wei without intermediate rounding At least the greater of 0.01 WETH or 100 bps of hedge cost
Price history 1,800-second TWAP; spot/TWAP difference at most 100 ticks Pool must serve the full window; otherwise fail
Freshness and inclusion Quote no older than one block; at least 36 seconds or three blocks remain, matching the report clock Simulation and private submission complete inside that budget
Operators Two independently funded and operated bots Both rehearse dispute, settlement, withdrawal, and rebalancing
  • Provision substantially deeper, durable REP/WETH liquidity or use an independently justified deeper reference and hedge venue.
  • Revisit minimum report sizing and the 5% target so security calculations include venue price impact, not only gas and protocol fees.
  • Fund at least two independent arbitragers and rehearse both dispute directions, settlement, withdrawal, and inventory rebalancing on a fork.
  • Rerun this fixed-block analysis plus stressed executable quotes immediately before deployment; fail closed if either direction is unprofitable.
  • Monitor liquidity, observation history, pending reports, quote profit, inclusion delay, and base-fee stress continuously after launch.

The root OpenOracle arbitrager automates event monitoring, TWAP filtering, executable quotes, state-hash revalidation, simulation, and optional dispute submission. It defaults to dry-run and correctly refuses the 5% cases above. It does not remove inclusion, locked-capital, or liquidity risk.
