AUCTION-A1 Launch Decision

Status: awaiting accountable human approval. This record makes the requested loss policy and monitoring explicit. It does not fabricate executive, risk-owner, or governance acceptance. Deployment is blocked until those owners sign the decision below.

Decision proposed for approval

Under AUCTION-A1, a truth auction is a best-effort repair mechanism. If demand is weak, it may clear below the child pool's ETH deficit. Finalization can activate an undercollateralized child pool. Users holding that child's open interest bear the remaining collateral impairment. Separately, non-migrating security positions forfeit the economic value assigned to qualifying auction buyers; they do not absorb the child pool's collateral deficit as a second liability. The protocol does not guarantee that auction buyers arrive, that the REP cap raises the target, or that governance recapitalizes the child. These are intentional and distinct loss allocations, not contract bugs.

Approval means accepting the concrete mechanics in Truth Auction clearing, including partial repair, weak-demand pricing, paged bidder settlement, and the absence of an automatic external backstop. It also accepts that different child universes can have materially different recovery rates.

Required pre-launch evidence

  • Fork rehearsal covering zero bids, one bid, oversubscribed bids, REP-cap binding, ETH-target binding, rounding dust, and paged claim completion.
  • Published child-pool deficit, target, REP cap, clearing price, retained ETH, post-repair collateral ratio, and unclaimed winning pages.
  • Named incident commander and communications owner for an underfunded child activation.
  • Operator runbook and alerts tested against a mainnet-fork deployment.
